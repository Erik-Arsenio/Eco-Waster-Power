Economic growth brings greater consumption by society and in turn greater accumulation of MSW as a secondary effect. With this fact comes new challenges and the need for implementing new solutions to reach and sustain the socio-economic goals.

In recent years the accumulation of MSW in the Caribbean and all Latin American countries has increased, generating more tons of garbage every day being deposited in open air landfill sites, and seriously affecting air, soil and water.

MSW accumulation represents one of the biggest challenges that any country could face in its economical development, and demands the best solution to deal with this destructive and never-ending menace.

One of the most beneficial decisions taken by developed nations to reduce the impact of MSW is the implementation of recycling and waste to energy technologies that:

  • Reduce contaminating gases.
  • Eliminate thousands tons of waste per year and reduce the need for landfills.
  • Generate electrical energy.
